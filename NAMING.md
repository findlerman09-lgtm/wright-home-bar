# Wright Home Bar Naming Standards

## 1. Established cocktails

Use the recognized historical or modern name exactly as published. Preserve established numbering where it is genuinely part of the drink's identity, such as **Corpse Reviver No. 2** or **Pimm's Cup No. 1**.

Do not invent additional numbered sequels merely because a house drink resembles an established recipe. Wright Home Bar should not create a "No. 7" simply to make a name sound historic.

## 2. Recognizable variations

Keep a descriptive family name when it helps the drinker understand what the drink is. Examples include:

- Passion Fruit Daiquiri
- Black Walnut Old Fashioned
- Cucumber Margarita
- Aquavit Gimlet

These names are useful rather than generic because they identify a familiar cocktail structure and the meaningful variation.

## 3. Wright House Originals

A genuinely original drink should receive a distinct, memorable name. Its principal ingredients or family should appear as a subtitle so the app remains searchable and understandable.

Example:

**The Conservatory**  
Gin, cucumber, basil and lime

House names should be short, pronounceable, and specific enough to remember. Avoid repetitive ingredient-pair names, vague luxury language, faux-historic numbering, and long strings of poetic adjectives.

## 4. Numbering rule

Numbering is allowed only when one of these is true:

1. The number is part of an established cocktail's recognized name.
2. The drinks form a documented and intentional house series with a clear shared concept.
3. The number itself has independent meaning, rather than merely distinguishing one recipe from another.

Otherwise, assign the drink its own name.

## 5. Provenance

Every recipe should be identified as one of the following:

- Established classic
- Modern classic
- Recognizable variation
- Wright House Original
- Experimental / cutting edge

House originals must not be presented as historical recipes.
